About Bucovina

Magical lands

Magica Bucovina, located in the northwest of Moldova, stands out for its legendary stories. The nature of modeled this area, turning it into an impressive pastel, full of nobility and solemnity, resembling a vast natural amphitheater descending in steps from west to east.

From the heights of the Călimani and Bistrita Mountains to the low region of the meadow, the attractions of Bucovina are inexhaustible. Parallel rows of hills, covered with imposing forests of fir, beech or spruce, sprinkled with meadows and hayfields that keep their green hue unchanged until the snow falls, follow each other like frozen waves stretching far into the distance.

I would never have thought of Bucovina as a perfect destination for active tourism, everyone knows that Bucovina is famous for its old painted churches and monasteries, UNESCO monuments, may precisely for religious tourism (pilgrimages or individual visits to monasteries).

If you're into rock climbing or archery parks, ziplines, swimming pools or lounging in the air free, Ariniș Amusement Park, together with its partners from Escalada Adventure Park, Agrement Kiralida, or Climbing Zone can offer you days full of adventures. Do you want to go hiking? You have countless marked routes, of various degrees of difficulty. Bucovina is a land full of countless attractions, from picturesque towns to spectacular Rarau Mountains, Călimani, Bistriței or Zugreni Gorges. Bucovina is also a land of natural reserves that house rare species of flowers (the meadows of Ponoare, the reserve of dwarf birch from Lucian or the bear grape from Moldova – Sulica), forestry (the secular forest of Slatioara, the secular forests of Giumalau and Zamostea) and geological (Pojorâta, Cheile Zugreni, 12 Apostoli Complex)

Bucovina is known throughout the world for the monasteries built by former rulers and boyars Moldovans at the end of the 15th and the beginning of the 16th century, each painted with its specific color: Voronet (blue), Humor (red), Sucevița (green), Moldovița (yellow), Tree (color combination). Eight of the painted churches were registered in the Unesco World Cultural Heritage (Voroneț, Humor, Moldovița, Sucevița, Suceava, Arbore, Pătrauti, Probota).
